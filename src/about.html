Background

My Story

My family has always been very musical. All of my immediate family members have learned to play an instrument. When I was eight, I had the opportunity to learn to play the violin. I have been playing ever since. I was in school orchestras from 6th grade to 12th grade. I learned a lot from my time in orchestra. I learned how to work with, lead, and learn from others. Orchestra is very collaborative. You have to work as a team to create a cohesive sound.

Playing the violin has also taught me how important it is to dedicate time to develop skills. It takes time to learn a new skill. I have currently played the violin for over a decade, but I know I still have room to improve. However, I am keenly aware that I have come a long way from where I was. I think it is important to acknowledge how far I have come, while making goals for where I want to be in the future.

Why UX?

I have always been interested in designing and creative pursuits. My first outlet of digital creativity started in elementary school creating Google Slideshows. I have made hundreds of slideshows for both academic and personal projects. However, my first experience with coding came later in my Freshman year of High School. I took a class called "Intro to Computer Science". In that class, I learned how to code in HTML and CSS which led me to fall in love with web design. This experience also convinced me to continue taking computer classes throughout high school.

In my senior year of high school, I decided that I wanted to go to Weber State University. At this point, I had not decided on a major. My older brother is a Computer Science major. He introduced me to UX design after learning about it in his college courses. When I saw Weber State University had Web and User Experience listed as a major, it intrigued me. I decided that UX was perfect for me as I looked into it further.

I have always felt there are two important aspects that I want my future career to have. The first aspect is helping people. UX allows me to do this as I design for accessibility and usability. The second aspect is the ability to be creative. Designing websites makes this possible as I create wire frames, prototypes, and other deliverables.

Design Style

I love to use vibrant colors when designing websites. In my opinion, it makes the designs stand out and be more appealing. I prioritize clearly defined and distinct sections in my designs. Users should be able to be intuitively connect titles to the related text or content. Information architecture is an important design aspect that I feel should be prioritized on all websites.

Accessibility is very important to me. I believe that websites should be easy for everyone to access regardless of their background, ability, or experience. Designing while being conscientious of details, such as high enough color contrast, help all users be able to obtain information and accomplish tasks using websites.

My favorite step of the design process is creating wireframes and prototypes. I enjoy creating the layout and concept for the website. The process of deciding how a user would navigate a website and how to best cater to the needs of a user is very interesting to me. I design with the user in mind by employing UX tools, such as personas, to ensure that the user is being advocated for.
