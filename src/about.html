Background

My Story

I lived in Roy Utah until I was 16. I currently live in Clinton Utah. I am the youngest of four. I have two older sisters and one older brother. One of my favorite ways to spend my time is being with my family.

My family has always been very musical. Everyone in my immediate family has learned to play an instrument. When I was eight, I had the opportunity to learn to play the violin. I have been playing ever since. I was in school orchestra from 6th grade to 12th grade. I learned a lot from my time in orchestra. I learned how to work with, lead, and learn from others. Orchestra is very collaborative. You have to work as a team to create a cohesive sound.

Playing the violin has also taught me how important it is to dedicate time to develop skills. It takes time to learn a new skill. The value of hard work cannot be understated. I have currently played the violin for over a decade and yet I know I still have room to improve. However, I also know I have come a long way from where I was. I think it is important to acknowledge how far I have come, while making goals for where I want to be in the future.

Why UX?

I have always been interested in designing and creative pursuits. My first outlet of digital creativity started in Elementary School creating Google Slideshows. I have made hundreds of slideshows for both academic and personal projects. I enjoyed making detailed and elaborate slideshows. However, my experience with coding came later in my Freshman year of High School. I took a class called "Intro to Computer Science". In that class I learned to code websites and fell in love with web design. This experience led me to continue taking computer classes throughout high school.

In my senior year of high school I decided that I wanted to go to Weber State University. At this point, I had not decided on a major. My older brother is a Computer Science major. He introduced me to UX design after learning about it in his college courses. When I saw Weber State University had Web and User Experience listed as a major, it intrigued me. I decided that UX was perfect for me as I looked into it further.

I have always felt there are two important aspects that I want my future career to have. I want to ability to help people. UX allows me to do this as I design for accessibility and usability. The second aspect I want is the ability to be creative. Designing websites makes this possible as I create wire frames, prototypes, and other deliverables.

Design Style

When it comes to designing websites, I love to use vibrant colors. In my opinion, it makes the designs stand out and be more appealing. When I design I prioritize ensuring that sections are clearly defined and distinct. Users should be able to be intuitively link titles to the related text or content. Information architecture is an important design aspect that I feel should be prioritized on all websites.

Accessibility is very important to me. I believe that websites should be easy for everyone to access regardless of their background, ability, or experience. Designing while being conscientious of details, such as high enough color contrast, help all users be able to obtain information and accomplish tasks using websites.

My favorite step of the design process is creating wireframes and prototypes. I enjoy creating the layout and concept for the website. The process of deciding how a user would navigate a website and how to best cater to the needs of a user is very interesting to me. I design with the user in mind by employing UX tools, such as personas, to ensure that the user is being advocated for.
